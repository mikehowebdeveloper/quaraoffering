Our Scientific Background
Quara ~ sensors for life is bringing that breakthrough device to market using its patented technology developed by the renowned scientific teams at Pebble Labs in Los Alamos, New Mexico and Optiens in Denver, Colorado. Pebble’s global team is run by Dr. Richard Sayre, who Nature has describes as one of the five people who will change the world. Optienz is overseen by Ken Reardon, Quara’s Chief Science Officer, who is Professor and Juged and Pat Harper Chair of Chemical and Biological Engineering and holds joint appointments in several other programs at Colorado State University, including Cell and Molecular Biology and Biomedical Engineering.

Why The World Needs a Handheld Early Infection Detection Device
Bacteria are essential to life – but bad bacteria can be costly and deadly $6 billion per year lost to disease in the global aquaculture industry Annual global cost of food-borne illness is estimated at over $110 billion and make over 48 million Americans stricken ill each year

Over 23,000 people die annually in the US from antibiotic resistant bacteria More than 700,000 people die from superbug bacterial infections every year

Superbugs expected to cost the global economy $100 trillion in health care and lost productivity by 2050 (as per the International Federation of Pharmaceutical Manufacturers & Associations, Nov 2018)

Current inadequate detection and control of bacteria costs billions in food production losses We over-use antibiotics, in our health care and food supply, in part because we do not detect infections early enough and lack timely feedback on a drugs’ effectiveness
